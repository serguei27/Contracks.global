The Problem We Solve

The portfolio isn't
ungovernable.
The system is missing.

Every Head of PMO we work with had plans. Primavera, Excel, slide decks — sometimes all three in parallel. When SteerCo gets uncomfortable, nobody can answer the one question that matters: "Which version is approved?"

1
No Enforceable Baseline
Five versions of "the plan" across Excel, Primavera, and slide decks. Every function defends their own dates in steering. Scope shifts in site conversations, then shows up later as surprises. Nobody can answer which version is approved without digging through email chains.
The portfolio becomes ungovernable. The PMO gets blamed for a system that was never installed.
2
Fuzzy Decision Rights
Decisions made in hallway chats, WhatsApp threads, or side calls with vendors — the PMO finds out after procurement has moved. SteerCo becomes status theatre. When things go wrong, everyone points at someone else.
Accountability collapses. The PMO loses authority and becomes a reporting function instead of a control tower.
3
Change Enters Unmanaged
"Quick tweaks" from engineering and country leads get actioned before impact is priced or sequenced. By the time RAID logs are updated, the ground truth has already moved. Budget creep, schedule churn, teams working weekends to catch up.
In 6–12 months the portfolio looks like a string of "unavoidable overruns" — and leadership starts replacing the PMO lead.
The Deep Fear
"I'm going to be the person everyone blames when this portfolio slips — not because I'm incompetent, but because I cannot prove what is true, I cannot force decisions to stick, and I cannot make leaders face reality."

How It Works

The Delivery Control
System™ — 5Cs

Five decisions applied in sequence. Not a governance framework — a practical control loop that makes the portfolio steerable while the job is running. Each C removes a specific failure mode. Together, they create one defensible version of the truth.
C
C1
Contain
Scope
Turn fuzzy requirements into written scope, documented assumptions, and clear acceptance criteria. Without this, everything is opinion. Every future change has a clean "before" to measure against.
C
C2

One Plan
Move from deck governance to one plan that matches site reality. Not a polished Primavera file nobody updates — one live baseline everyone actually runs. No version confusion. No parallel plans.

Stop RAID drift. Every open item gets an owner, a deadline, and a consequence. Decisions made once — recorded, published fast, tied to named actions. Not reopened the following week.

Common Questions

Objections we hear.
Answers that hold.

"Won't this add more bureaucracy and slow delivery?"
The controls are designed to fit around live delivery, not stop it. The Change Intake Gate takes minutes to process. The Decision Rights Map reduces the volume of alignment meetings. Most clients report fewer rework cycles within the first two weeks — because changes are priced before they start, not after.
"This will die when the consultant leaves."
That's why the Handover and Adoption System is a core deliverable, not an afterthought. The templates, standards, and meeting scripts are designed to be run by your team without ongoing support. We don't leave until the rhythm is running without us in the room.
"We're already doing governance. What's different here?"
Most governance is decorative — gates that get skipped, decision logs nobody updates, baselines in Primavera that the site ignores. The Delivery Control System™ is mechanically enforced: change doesn't enter without a ticket, dates don't move without an impact conversation, decisions don't get made outside the forum. The difference is teeth.
"Our programme is too complex / too political for a standard system."
We have installed this across a GCC metro project during a geopolitical embargo, a 20-year multi-stakeholder real estate portfolio, and simultaneous cross-border pipelines in two countries. Complexity and politics are the environment, not the obstacle. The system is designed for exactly that pressure.
"Six weeks seems short. Can you really install something that sticks in that time?"
The six weeks installs the system on one live programme, not the entire organisation. Phase 1 locks the baseline. Phase 2 gates the change. Phase 3 makes decisions stick. By week six, the rhythm is running and the team owns it. The Handover System ensures it scales beyond week six without us.
"What happens after the six weeks?"
Three options: your team runs the system independently using the Handover Package. We stay on as a Claims Triage Retainer (£8,500/month) for ongoing commercial defence. Or we scale the methodology across your portfolio with the Steerable Portfolio Control Tower engagement. The six-week install is the entry point, not the ceiling.
