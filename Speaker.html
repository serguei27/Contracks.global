Topics

No-Surprises Delivery
Why projects don't fail — the systems around them do
Even with templates, stage gates, RAID logs and reports, projects still drift: scope shifts, governance disconnects from how work gets done, and vendor slippage turns delivery into a constant chase. This keynote shows PMO Directors and project leaders how to make controls match reality — using the Delivery Control System™ to align requirements, procurement, contracts and execution, so decisions get enforced rather than argued.
Beyond Paperwork Governance
When the official plan and the site stop telling the same story
Governance looks right on paper — but the site runs on WhatsApp, verbal agreements, and supplier dates nobody verified. Theory looks good. Reality drifts. Drawing on 30 countries of cross-border project work, this talk names the gap, explains why it widens fastest in frontier and high-pressure environments, and shows the five decisions that close it.
Claims-Proof Delivery
When time becomes money — and how to be ready
Most EPC and infrastructure claims fail not because the cause was weak, but because the timeline was never built to defend it. This session unpacks the Claims-Proof Timeline System™ — the 5Ls — and shows commercial managers how to log, link and load a defensible position from day one, not the week before notification.

Bio

Serguei Poppeleer is the founder of Contracks Global and creator of the Delivery Control System™ — a method for turning the person accountable for delivery into the person who actually delivers, in the markets where standard playbooks break first.

25+ years across 30+ countries. In Equatorial Guinea he scrapped the imported playbook, rebuilt delivery around ground reality, local entrepreneurs and community relationships — and delivered the camp in 6 months instead of 12, at one-tenth the cost, with zero security incidents.

He has worked with ENI · SNAM RETE · EnergiNet · ArcelorMittal · Brussels Airlines · Intels Nigeria · Marriott · Luminus, on projects from €100m to €700m. He speaks English, French, Dutch and Italian.
