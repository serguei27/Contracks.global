Pain Points

The Challenge — Sound Familiar?

Slide Content
The Challenge:
  • You have 5 versions of "the plan" across Excel, Primavera/MS Project, and slide decks — nobody can answer "which version is approved?"
  • Decisions get made in hallway chats, WhatsApp threads, or side calls with vendors — SteerCo becomes status theatre and everyone points fingers.
  • Change requests arrive as "quick tweaks" and get actioned before impact is priced or sequenced — teams work weekends to catch up.

Sound familiar?

Presenter Notes

These three problems usually show up as one tangled knot. On the surface it looks like "we need better reporting" or "we need the PMO to tighten governance." Underneath, it's simpler and more dangerous: there is no single enforceable baseline, so every conversation becomes negotiable; decision rights are fuzzy, so real choices happen in side channels; and change enters unmanaged, so the portfolio keeps shifting while the dashboard tries to describe yesterday.

In real situations this sounds like: "Can someone send me the latest schedule?" followed by three different files, then 20 minutes arguing about which one is real. Or a vendor shows up saying "We already agreed this on the call," and the PMO is left digging through WhatsApp screenshots while procurement has already moved.

The commercial impact is brutal (budget creep, schedule churn, lender/executive confidence dropping). The emotional impact is worse: you walk into SteerCo knowing you're going to be challenged on numbers you can't fully defend. And the lifestyle impact is what people don't say out loud — weekends spent reconciling plans, rewriting decks, and chasing approvals that should have been clean in the first place.
