Questions before you book

What the Reset is — and what it is not.

Six answers for programme owners who need control fast, without launching a six-month consulting exercise.

Can ten days really fix a drifting programme?

Not completely — and the Reset does not pretend to. Ten days is enough to identify where control has been lost, prove the gaps with evidence, rank the recoverable risks, and produce a 30/60/90-day recovery plan. The deeper fix is the Turnaround.

What if our programme is too complex or political for a standard framework?

The Reset is structured, not generic. The 5Cs give the assessment sequence, but the evidence comes from your live programme: scope, plan, decisions, vendor dates and cadence. The method has been used across crisis conditions, cross-border programmes and politically sensitive delivery environments.

What do you need from my team?

Access to the core documents, the current plan, the decision log or equivalent, selected vendor dates, and a small number of focused interviews. Delivery does not stop. The Reset is designed to work around the programme, not consume it.

What if this only confirms what we already know?

Then the value is turning what people feel into evidence leaders can act on. Many PMO teams know the programme is drifting, but cannot prove where, sequence the recovery, or defend the case to SteerCo. The Reset converts concern into an executive-ready control narrative.

What happens after the Reset?

You can run the 30/60/90-day plan yourself, ask Contracks Global to support the Decision-Ready Programme Turnaround, or retain advisory support for cadence and control. There is no forced retainer.

Is this for contractors or owners?

The Reset is built for owner-side teams: PMO Directors, programme owners, sponsors and executives accountable for delivery. It can involve contractors and vendors, but the mandate is to restore owner-side control.
